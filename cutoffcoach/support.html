Caffeine intake tracking and sleep cutoff time prediction (iOS)

CutoffCoach helps you log your caffeine intake and tells you the latest time you should stop drinking caffeine so it does not disrupt your sleep. This page answers common questions. If you need more help, email us anytime.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does "cutoff" mean?

Your cutoff time is the latest point in the day at which you can have caffeine without it likely interfering with your target bedtime. The App estimates this based on how much caffeine you have logged, your body weight, and your caffeine sensitivity setting, then works out when enough of that caffeine should have cleared your system before sleep.

Where is my data stored?

All of your data is stored only on your device. CutoffCoach has no accounts and no sign-up, and it does not send your personal data to any server. If you delete the App, that data is removed with it. For details, see our Privacy Policy.

Can I remove the ads?

CutoffCoach is currently free and supported by banner ads through Google AdMob. There is no paid ad-removal option yet. If we add one in a future update, it will appear in the App. On iOS you can choose whether to allow ad tracking when the App asks; declining still lets you use every feature, you will just see non-personalized ads.

My drink isn't in the list. What do I do?

You can log caffeine manually. When a drink is not in the built-in list, use the manual / custom entry option to type in the caffeine amount (in milligrams) yourself. Check the product label or the maker's website for the exact caffeine content, then enter that value so your cutoff time stays accurate.

Are the recommendations medical advice?

No. CutoffCoach is for informational purposes only and its predictions are estimates, not medical advice. Consult a qualified healthcare provider for any health-related decisions.
